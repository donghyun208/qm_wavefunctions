The particle on a ring problem is a two dimensional particle that is constrained such that it can only exist on a ring of radius $R$. After a change of variables from $(x,y)$ to $(r,\theta)$, we would find that the Hamiltonian has no dependence on $r$, and that the problem can be interpreted as a free particle in 1 dimension with periodic boundary conditions.
$$ \begin{align*} \hat{H} &= -\frac{\hbar^2}{2mR^2}\frac{\mathrm{d}^2}{\mathrm{d}\theta^2} & \psi(\theta) &= \psi(2\pi + \theta) \end{align*} $$
