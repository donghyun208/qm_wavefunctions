Time Evolution


The equation that governs time evolution in quantum mechanics is the Time Dependent Schrodinger Equation: $$ i\hbar\frac{\partial}{\partial t} \Psi(r,t) = \hat{H}\Psi(r,t) $$ For time-independent Hamiltonians, the general solution is given as: $$ \Psi(r,t) = e^{-\frac{i}{\hbar}\hat{H}t}\Psi(r,0)\\ $$ where $\Psi(r,0)$ is the initial condition. This means if we know $\Psi(r,0)$, the state of our system at time $t=0$, and we know the Hamiltonian, we can know what the state of the system at any future time as well.
