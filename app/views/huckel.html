Huckel Theory

We will write the Hamiltonian in a basis of $\phi_{p_z}$ atomic orbitals. For benzene, we only include the valence orbital from each of the six atoms: $\{\phi_1, \phi_2, \phi_3, \phi_4, \phi_5, \phi_6\}$. We define the following integrals: $$\langle\phi_i|H|\phi_i\rangle = \alpha \qquad \langle\phi_i|\phi_i\rangle = \delta_{i,j}$$ If $\phi_i$ and $\phi_j$ are neighboring atoms, then $$\langle\phi_i|H|\phi_j\rangle = \beta$$
$$ H = \left[ \begin{array}{cccccc} \alpha & \beta & 0 & 0 & 0 & \beta \\ \beta & \alpha & \beta & 0 & 0 & 0\\ 0 & \beta & \alpha & \beta & 0 & 0\\ 0 & 0 & \beta & \alpha & \beta & 0\\ 0 & 0 & 0 & \beta & \alpha & \beta\\ \beta & 0 & 0 & 0 & \beta & \alpha \end{array} \right] $$

Degenerate Eigenstates

$\psi_1$ and $\psi_6$ have unique eigenvalues, but $\psi_2$ and $\psi_3$ are degenerate with the eigenvalue $\alpha + \beta$, and $\psi_4$ and $\psi_5$ are degenerate with the eigenvalue $\alpha - \beta$. We can prove that linear combinations of degenerate eigenstates are also valid eigenstates of the Hamiltonian: $$ \begin{align} H\psi_\text{mix} &= H(c_2\psi_2 + c_3\psi_3)\\ &= c_2 H\psi_2 + c_3 H\psi_3\\ &= c_2(\alpha + \beta)\psi_2 + c_3(\alpha + \beta)\psi_3\\ &= (\alpha + \beta)(c_2\psi_2 + c_3\psi_3)\\ H\psi_\text{mix} &= (\alpha + \beta)\psi_\text{mix} \end{align} $$ Slide the mix angle bar to see how the degenerate eigenstates mix. Notice what happens every $30^\circ$. We will find that for degenerate perturbation theory, the correct zeroth order wavefunctions to use are the ones with the proper mixing angle such that the degenerate energy splitting is maximized.
